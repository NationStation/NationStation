Executive Summary

Nation Station implements comprehensive food security programs for vulnerable families in Beirut, Lebanon. In 2025, we operated two parallel food security programs: a Fondation de France-funded project serving 90 families (485 individuals) with 100% completion, and an OCHA/LHF-funded program implemented jointly with Mada Association reaching 550 households (2,530 people) in Beirut's Southern Suburbs. Combined, these programs have supported 640 families and 3,015 individuals across multiple neighborhoods in Beirut.

Hard-to-Reach Area: Beirut Southern Suburbs (BSS)

Since the 2024–2025 conflict, Beirut's Southern Suburbs (BSS) have become one of the most socio-economically vulnerable and politically sensitive areas in Lebanon. Humanitarian operations there require strict coordination with the Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR) unit, which oversees all aid entering these neighborhoods.

Through an ongoing UN OCHA–funded food assistance program implemented with Mada Association, Nation Station obtained DRR clearance and established compliant, conflict-sensitive access to BSS. As part of this program, the DRR shared a verified beneficiary dataset for BSS with our team, including household profiles and vulnerability information.

Combined with additional wartime interventions with the same donor, we now manage a large, accurate and up-to-date database of vulnerable households in BSS. This dataset underpins both our current food security work and our future digital resilience planning.

Vulnerability Criteria: How We Identify Those Most in Need

We employ a rigorous, data-driven assessment process to identify the most vulnerable families. Our vulnerability criteria are based on internationally recognized food security indicators and economic measures:

Economic Vulnerability

  • 73.3% of assessed households have monthly incomes below the extreme poverty line
  • 58.9% reported reducing meal sizes in the month prior to assessment due to lack of resources
  • 95.6% rely primarily on cash purchases for food, making them highly vulnerable to price inflation
  • 22% depend on assistance from organizations as their secondary food source

Food Security Indicators

  • Food Consumption Score (FCS): Measures dietary diversity and frequency of food consumption
  • Coping Strategy Index (CSI): Tracks negative behaviors families adopt to cope with food shortages
  • Household composition: Special consideration for households with infants, young children, pregnant/lactating women, and elderly members
  • Displacement status: Priority for internally displaced and war-affected families

In BSS, these vulnerability criteria are applied using the DRR/OCHA-validated dataset combined with our own digital assessments (income, food security scores, household composition and special needs). This dual approach minimizes inclusion and exclusion errors and helps us prioritize the most at-risk households.

Accountability & Protection Against Misuse

Nation Station implements comprehensive safeguards to ensure aid reaches intended beneficiaries and funds are used appropriately:

Digital Verification System

We use our own digital integrated platforms to conduct comprehensive household assessments, capturing:

  • Household composition and demographics
  • Income levels and sources
  • Current food sources and consumption patterns
  • Specific nutritional needs

All data is digitally recorded, time-stamped, and geo-tagged for verification.

Our small in-house digital & AI lab supports this system: it maintains and cleans the beneficiary database (including DRR/OCHA data for BSS), designs Kobo/ODK forms and simple dashboards, runs the OTP and WhatsApp communication flows where needed, and aggregates complaints and feedback for course correction.

Direct Distribution Model

Food assistance is delivered directly to families without intermediaries:

  • Identity verification at distribution points
  • Individual labeling of food boxes with recipient information
  • Beneficiary confirmation and signature collection
  • No cash transfers or vouchers that could be diverted

This eliminates opportunities for diversion to armed groups or other entities.

Monitoring & Documentation

Comprehensive tracking and accountability measures:

  • Photographic documentation of all preparation and distribution activities
  • Detailed inventory tracking of purchased food items
  • Distribution records with beneficiary confirmation
  • Regular team meetings to address operational challenges
  • Feedback collection from beneficiaries
Additional Safeguards

Our team operates from the Nation Station facility in Geitawi Area, Ashrafieh, Beirut, a secure and neutral location. We maintain strict political neutrality and do not engage with armed groups. All procurement is done through established commercial suppliers with proper documentation, and our staff members are community-based individuals with no affiliations to armed organizations.

These controls – combined with DRR oversight in BSS, our political neutrality, and exclusive use of vetted commercial suppliers – are designed to prevent any diversion of assistance or funds to armed groups.

Innovative Approach: Gender and Age-Sensitive Food Allocation

A key innovation of our 2025 project was the tailored food box design that considered the demographic composition of each household. This approach maximized nutritional impact and demonstrated our commitment to needs-based, dignified assistance.

Infants (0-3 years)
  • 30-40% of adult portions
  • No high-salt items
  • No mortadella
  • Limited sugar
  • Infant formula included where needed
Children (3-9 years)
  • 60-80% of adult portions
  • Reduced spices
  • Increased nutrient density
  • Age-appropriate food items
Adults & Elderly
  • Standard portions
  • Gender-specific nutritional needs considered
  • Special items for women of reproductive age
  • Dietary diversity ensured

Parallel OCHA/LHF Food Security Program with Mada Association

In parallel to the 2025 Fondation de France project, an OCHA/LHF-funded food security program, implemented by Nation Station and Mada Association, uses the Nation Station warehouse in Geitawi as its main operational hub for Beirut's Southern Suburbs (BSS).

  • Tender reference: ITB-OCHAIV2025BT01.
  • Scale: Approximately 550 households (around 2,530 people) in BSS.
  • Operations: Boxes/food parcels are prepared and dispatched through the Geitawi warehouse under UN OCHA compliance requirements.

This parallel pipeline demonstrates that the same logistics platform can safely support larger caseloads in a politically sensitive area while remaining aligned with UN OCHA standards for procurement, warehousing, and distribution.
